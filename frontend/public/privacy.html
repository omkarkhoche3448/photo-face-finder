Privacy Policy

Last Updated: November 22, 2025

1. Introduction

Welcome to Photo Extractor. We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data. This privacy policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use our service.

Photo Extractor allows you to upload reference photos and scan your friends' Google Photos libraries to find matching photos. This policy describes how we handle your data throughout this process.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide

  • Reference Photos: Photos you upload to create face detection profiles
  • Google Account Information: Email address and profile information when you authenticate with Google

2.2 Information We Collect Automatically

  • Google Photos Data: When you authorize our app, we temporarily access your Google Photos library to scan for matching faces
  • OAuth Tokens: Encrypted access tokens to access your Google Photos (stored securely)
  • Matched Photos: Photos from your Google Photos library that match the uploaded reference faces
  • Technical Data: IP addresses, browser type, device information, and usage logs

2.3 Face Detection Data

  • Face Embeddings: Mathematical representations of faces extracted from reference photos (not the photos themselves)
  • Confidence Scores: Similarity scores indicating how well a photo matches the reference face

3. How We Use Your Information

3.1 Primary Purpose

  • To scan Google Photos libraries for faces matching your uploaded reference photos
  • To generate face embeddings for comparison
  • To identify and extract matched photos
  • To create shareable links for photo extraction sessions

3.2 Service Operations

  • To authenticate you via Google OAuth
  • To store matched photos securely in AWS S3
  • To provide download links for matched photos
  • To track scanning progress and status

3.3 Communication

  • To send you scan completion notifications (if enabled)
  • To respond to your support requests
  • To notify you of important service updates

4. How We Store and Protect Your Data

4.1 Data Storage

  • OAuth Tokens: Encrypted using AES-256 encryption and stored in our secure PostgreSQL database
  • Matched Photos: Stored in AWS S3 with restricted access and encryption at rest
  • Face Embeddings: Stored securely in our database, encrypted
  • Session Data: Stored in Redis cache with automatic expiration (24 hours)

4.2 Data Security

  • All data transmission uses HTTPS/TLS encryption
  • OAuth tokens are encrypted and never exposed in logs
  • Access to servers and databases is restricted to authorized personnel only
  • Regular security audits and updates

4.3 What We DON'T Store

  • We do NOT store your entire Google Photos library
  • We do NOT store unmatched photos
  • We do NOT permanently cache Google Photos metadata beyond the scanning session
  • We do NOT share your photos with third parties

5. Data Retention

5.1 Retention Periods

  • Matched Photos: Stored until you request deletion or after 90 days of inactivity
  • OAuth Tokens: Stored until you revoke access or delete your data
  • Face Embeddings: Stored for the duration of the session (30 days by default)
  • Session Data: Automatically deleted after 30 days
  • Logs: Retained for 30 days for security and debugging purposes

5.2 Automatic Deletion

Sessions and associated data are automatically deleted after 30 days of inactivity. OAuth tokens are automatically revoked and deleted when a session expires.

6. Data Sharing and Disclosure

6.1 We Do NOT Sell Your Data

We do not and will never sell, rent, or trade your personal information or photos to third parties.

6.2 Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services:

  • Google OAuth: For authentication and Google Photos access (subject to Google's Privacy Policy)
  • AWS S3: For secure photo storage (subject to AWS Privacy Policy)
  • Cloud Hosting: For application infrastructure

6.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required by law or to:

  • Comply with legal processes or government requests
  • Enforce our Terms of Service
  • Protect our rights, property, or safety
  • Investigate fraud or security issues

7. Your Rights and Choices

7.1 Access and Control

  • Access Your Data: You can view all your matched photos and session data
  • Download Your Data: You can download all matched photos at any time
  • Delete Your Data: You can request complete deletion of your data
  • Revoke Access: You can revoke Google Photos access at any time

7.2 How to Exercise Your Rights

  • To delete your data, click "Delete All Data" in the scan results page
  • To revoke Google Photos access, visit your Google Account Permissions
  • For data export or other requests, contact us at the email below

7.3 Data Deletion

When you request data deletion, we will permanently delete:

  • All matched photos from AWS S3
  • All face embeddings and session data
  • OAuth tokens from our database
  • All associated metadata and logs

Deletion is permanent and cannot be undone. We will complete deletion within 30 days of your request.

8. Google Photos API Usage

Our use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

8.1 Limited Use Disclosure

  • We only access Google Photos data you explicitly authorize
  • We use Google Photos data solely for face matching and photo extraction
  • We do not use Google Photos data for advertising or marketing
  • We do not use Google Photos data to build user profiles

8.2 Scope of Access

We request the following Google Photos scopes:

  • photoslibrary.readonly: Read-only access to your photos for scanning
  • userinfo.email: Your email address for account identification
  • userinfo.profile: Your basic profile information

9. Cookies and Tracking

9.2 Analytics
